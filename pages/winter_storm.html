Emergency Preparedness Bag

The Winter Storm Bag

During winter months it might be good to have a few things extra packed in your bag. The temperatures get a lot lower during these months and with the extra gear for winter you're going to want a bigger bag. The 75 litre Gregory Denali from MEC should have what it takes to carry your needs. Click the bag.

  • Item List
  • Emergency plan.
  • Two days worth of food and water.
  • Toiletries.
  • Flashlight with extra batteries.
  • Fire starter (lighter, paper, paste).
  • Emergency blanket.
  • Flares.
  • Change of clothes.
  • Copies of all your important information (birth certificate, identification, house/insurance papers and more).
  • First Aid kit.
  • Charger for your phone and smart devices (solar option).
  • Extra cash.
  • Flagging ribbon and a marker.
  • Pen and paper.
  • A sleeping bag for below zero degrees.
  • Toque/winter hat and wool socks (breathable material will be what helps. You don't want anything that's going to hold moisture and make you sweat) with an extra pair of socks.
  • Hot paws to help warm up your hands and feet.
  • Warm Jacket.
  • Gloves (I find the big mittens work best. The more space between the skin on your hands and the air the better. Finger gloves work well too for mobility but are not as effective).
  • Depending how deep the snow is and how much moving around you are doing. You may want a set of snowshoes and ski poles to help trek through snow.
  • Slip on ice cleats.
  • Polar sunglasses to help with snow blinding.
  • Axe.
  • Small foldable shovel.
