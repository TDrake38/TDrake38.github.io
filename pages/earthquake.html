Emergency Preparedness Bags

The Earthquake Bag

Let's talk about earthquakes and tornados. When these disasters happen there tends to be lots of rubble. Houses usually take a lot of damage and you are not left with a lot. We recommend the Gregory Stout 35 Backpack. A 35L bag. This is a good starter bag for any EPB. You won't feel too restricted on space and it won't be too heavy for long hikes. Click the bag.

  • Item List
  • Emergency plan.
  • Two days worth of food and water.
  • Toiletries.
  • Flash light.
  • Fire starter (lighter, paper, paste).
  • Change of clothes.
  • Copies of all your important information (birth certificate, identification, house/insurance papers and more).
  • First Aid kit.
  • Charger for your phone and smart devices (solar option).
  • Extra cash.
  • Pen and paper.
  • Dust masks.
  • A radio. One with a crank because you may not be able to get batteries very often.
  • Sleeping bag.
  • Knife or a multitool.
  • Duck tape, rope and plastic to make a shelter. Or a small tent.
