Emergency Preparedness Bags

Emergencies

Before we get into which bag would be good for which emergancy. Lets talk about what options you may want in a bag. Your going to want bottom commpression straps to take the weight and adjudtible shoulder harness for thoes long walks your going to take. These distribute the weight eveninly on your body so you don't bare so much of it on your shoulders. You may want a bag with some type of rain cover depending on where you live. Also anything under a 35L bag may not be enough to get you through a long period. That being said on to some essentials.

No matter which emergency happens to you. Every EPB (Emergency Preparedness Bag) should include the followng:

  • Emergency plan.
  • Two days worth of food and water.
  • Toiletries.
  • Flash light.
  • Fire starter (lighter, paper, paste).
  • Change of clothes.
  • Copies of all your important information (birth certificate, identification, house/insurance papers and more).
  • First Aid kit.
  • Charger for your phone and smart devices (solar option).
  • Extra cash.
  • Pen and paper.

Earthquakes/Tornadoes

Lets talk earthquakes and tornados. When these disasters happen there tends to be lots of rubble. Houses usually take a lot of damage and may not even make it through the event. In this case we reccomend this bag below with a few extra items.


This is the Gregory Stout 35 Backpack. A 35L bag. This is a good starter bag for any EPB. You wont feel restricted on space and it wont be too heavy for long hikes.

Extra items:

  • Dust masks.
  • A radio. One with a crank becuase you may not be able to get batteries very often.
  • Sleeping bag.
  • Knife or a multitool.
  • Ducktape, rope and plastic to make a shelter. Or a small tent.

Floods/Tsunamis

With a flood or a tsunami usually comes a lot of water. In this case your going to want a little bit bigger of a bag to hold a few more articles of clothing.

This Black Diamond Mission 55 Backpack. A 55L bag. With the extra space there is lots of room to keep things dry.

Extra items:

  • Water proof pouch for your important documents.
  • Flare(s).
  • Whistel.
  • Water proof matches.
  • Knife or can opener.
  • Ducktape, rope and plastic to make a shelter. Or a small tent.
  • Air mattress (can help with floating).

House/Wild Fires

When it comes to fires you will need to have this bag in an easy and accesible place. They can happen around the holidays or in the summer months when the heat is dry and there hasnt been much rain.

This is a 45L bag.

Extra items:

  • Knife or hatchet.
  • Plastic and tape to make a shelter (tent).
  • Usb with important computer documents.
  • Batteries.
  • Radio (crank if possible).
  • Sleeping bag.

Hurricane

Hurricanes are not fun. They come with a lot of wind and water. Like the flood/tsunami bag. You may need items to make a shelter if it takes your home.

This is a 38L bag.

Extra items:

  • Knife and a can opener.
  • Plastic and tape to make a shelter.
  • A radio. One with a crank becuase you may not be able to get batteries very often.
  • Photo of your loved ones.
  • Water proof matches.
  • Water proof pouch for your important documents.

Winter Months

During winter months it might be good to have a few things extra packed in your bag. The temperatures get a lot lower. Here are some times that you could pick up to help with the cold.

  • Sleeping bag good for below zero degrees.
  • Tooks and whool socks (breathable material will be what helps. You dont want anything thats going to hold in moisture and make you sweat).
  • Hot paws to help warm up your hands and feet.
  • Warm Jacket.
  • Gloves (I find the big mittons work best. The more space between the skin on your hands and the air the better. Figure gloves work well too for mobility but arnt as effective).
  • Depending on much moving around your doing you may want a set of snow shoes and ski poles to help treck through snow.
